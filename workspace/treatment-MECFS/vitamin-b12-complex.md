# Vitamin B12 and B Complex for ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-27
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

B vitamins — particularly vitamin B12 (cobalamin), B6 (pyridoxine), B9 (folate), and the broader B complex — play essential roles in energy metabolism, red blood cell formation, neurological function, and DNA synthesis. Vitamin B12, in particular, is critical for mitochondrial function and myelin sheath maintenance. In the ME/CFS community, B12 supplementation is extremely popular, often in the form of sublingual methylcobalamin or intramuscular B12 injections. The rationale includes addressing potential B12 deficiency due to poor diet or malabsorption, and the theoretical role of B12 in supporting cellular energy production and reducing oxidative stress.

## Scientific Findings

### Update — 2026-05-26
**Study:** *Underuse of Pharmacologic Therapies for Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome Before Specialist Evaluation* (PMID: [42055743](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42055743/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-MECFS/2026-05-26/42055743-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- In a retrospective study of 571 patients referred to an ME/CFS specialty clinic (2018–2022), vitamin B12 and B complex were the second most commonly reported supplements, after vitamin D.
- 72.2% of patients reported taking at least one dietary supplement, with B vitamins being among the top three categories (alongside vitamin D and fish oil).
- The high prevalence of B12/B complex use among ME/CFS patients suggests widespread patient-driven efforts to manage fatigue and neurological symptoms through supplementation.
- The study posits that the high reliance on supplements like B vitamins may reflect a treatment gap — patients seeking symptom relief from readily available supplements when prescription pharmacotherapy is not being offered by their primary care providers.
