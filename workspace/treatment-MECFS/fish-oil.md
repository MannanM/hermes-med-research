# Fish Oil (Omega-3 Fatty Acids) for ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-27
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Fish oil is a dietary supplement rich in omega-3 fatty acids, primarily eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA) and docosahexaenoic acid (DHA). These long-chain polyunsaturated fatty acids have well-documented anti-inflammatory properties and play important roles in cardiovascular health, brain function, and cell membrane integrity. In the context of ME/CFS, fish oil is of interest because chronic inflammation and immune dysfunction are hypothesized to contribute to symptoms. Some patients use fish oil as part of a broader anti-inflammatory or mitochondrial support protocol. It is also commonly used alongside CoQ10 and other mitochondrial-supporting supplements.

## Scientific Findings

### Update — 2026-05-26
**Study:** *Underuse of Pharmacologic Therapies for Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome Before Specialist Evaluation* (PMID: [42055743](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42055743/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-MECFS/2026-05-26/42055743-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- In a retrospective study of 571 patients referred to an ME/CFS specialty clinic (2018–2022), fish oil was among the top three most commonly used supplements, alongside vitamin D and B vitamins.
- 72.2% of patients reported having taken at least one dietary supplement before specialist evaluation, with fish oil being the third most common category.
- The widespread use of fish oil in this patient population aligns with a broader pattern: in the absence of accessible prescription pharmacotherapy, patients turn to supplements with anti-inflammatory and general health-promoting properties.
- The study's findings support the hypothesis that the high supplement use in ME/CFS may reflect unmet treatment needs rather than strong evidence for supplement efficacy.
