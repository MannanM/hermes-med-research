# Gabapentin for ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-27
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Gabapentin is a medication originally developed as an anticonvulsant but most commonly prescribed for nerve pain (neuropathic pain), fibromyalgia, and certain types of seizures. It works by modulating calcium channels in the nervous system, which reduces the release of neurotransmitters involved in pain signaling. For ME/CFS patients, gabapentin is often used off-label to manage chronic pain, muscle aches, and sometimes sleep disturbances. It is considered a first-line option by some ME/CFS specialists for pain management, particularly when patients cannot tolerate tricyclic antidepressants or SNRIs.

## Scientific Findings

### Update — 2026-05-26
**Study:** *Underuse of Pharmacologic Therapies for Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome Before Specialist Evaluation* (PMID: [42055743](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42055743/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-MECFS/2026-05-26/42055743-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- In a retrospective study of 571 patients referred to an ME/CFS specialty clinic, gabapentin was the second most commonly prescribed medication class potentially for ME/CFS, after SNRIs.
- The study categorized gabapentin among medications used for pain management in ME/CFS, alongside tricyclic antidepressants.
- Gabapentin's use reflects the high prevalence of chronic pain in ME/CFS and the recognition of pain as a core symptom requiring dedicated pharmacotherapy.
- Despite being relatively common among those who received any medication, the overall rate of pharmacotherapy for ME/CFS was limited (68.3%), suggesting gabapentin is still underprescribed relative to specialist recommendations.
