# Vitamin D for ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-27
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Vitamin D is a fat-soluble vitamin that plays essential roles in calcium absorption, bone health, immune function, and inflammation regulation. It is produced in the skin in response to sunlight and is also obtained from dietary sources and supplements. In the context of ME/CFS, vitamin D is of interest because many patients report low energy levels, and vitamin D deficiency is associated with fatigue, muscle weakness, and immune dysfunction — symptoms that overlap with ME/CFS. Vitamin D supplementation is one of the most commonly used supplements in the ME/CFS community, though research on its specific efficacy for ME/CFS symptoms remains limited.

## Scientific Findings

### Update — 2026-05-26
**Study:** *Underuse of Pharmacologic Therapies for Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome Before Specialist Evaluation* (PMID: [42055743](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42055743/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-MECFS/2026-05-26/42055743-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- In a retrospective study of 571 patients referred to an ME/CFS specialty clinic (2018–2022), vitamin D was the most commonly reported dietary supplement taken by patients before specialist evaluation.
- 72.2% of patients reported having taken at least one supplement, and vitamin D was the single most common among them.
- The high prevalence of vitamin D use may reflect both general health awareness and patients' efforts to self-manage fatigue and immune symptoms in the absence of accessible prescription pharmacotherapy.
- The study suggests that the high rate of supplement use (including vitamin D) may be driven, at least in part, by the underprescription of ME/CFS-specific medications by general practitioners.
