# Plain Language Summary: Clinical and Biomechanical Biomarkers in Lumbar Spinal Stenosis

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42171737](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42171737/) | **Published:** 2026-05-22
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42171737.md) | [Full Text](./42171737-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This multicenter study examined factors associated with patient satisfaction after lumbar spinal stenosis (LCS) surgery, analyzing data from 241 patients who completed questionnaires before surgery and at 6-month and 1-year follow-ups.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** About 70% of patients were satisfied with their LCS surgery at both 6 months and 1 year post-operatively.
- **Finding 2:** Nearly half (47.6%) of patients who were dissatisfied at 6 months improved by 1 year, suggesting recovery continues beyond 6 months.
- **Finding 3:** Younger age and psychological distress were independent predictors of dissatisfaction after LCS surgery. Better preoperative psychological health was associated with better surgical satisfaction.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
Lumbar spinal stenosis is a concern for hypermobility patients, particularly those with associated spinal degeneration. This study reinforces that psychological factors and expectations significantly influence surgical outcomes — an important consideration when making shared decisions about spine surgery.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Lumbar decompression surgery** — relieving pressure on spinal nerves
- **Lumbar spinal fusion** — for patients with spondylolisthesis
