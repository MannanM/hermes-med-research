# Plain Language Summary: Surgical Management of Posterolateral Rotatory Instability of the Elbow

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42171819](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42171819/) | **Published:** 2026-05-22
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42171819.md) | [Full Text](./42171819-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This study describes a surgical technique for treating recurrent elbow instability (posterolateral rotatory instability, PLRI) in patients who also have bone defects in the capitellum (the rounded end of the upper arm bone where it meets the elbow). Five patients underwent an innovative procedure using a small metal resurfacing prosthesis originally designed for toe joints, combined with ligament reconstruction.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** All five patients achieved excellent clinical outcomes at a median 5-year follow-up, with no complications or need for reoperation.
- **Finding 2:** One patient had marfanoid habitus and generalized joint laxity — a presentation relevant to connective tissue disorders.
- **Finding 3:** The study demonstrates that addressing both the ligament and bone components of elbow instability is essential for successful treatment.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
Elbow instability is a known problem in hypermobility/Ehlers-Danlos patients. This study is particularly relevant because one of the five patients had generalized joint laxity (marfanoid habitus). The key clinical message: when treating recurrent elbow instability, surgeons should look for and address any bone defects in the capitellum — simply repairing the ligament may not be enough if a bone defect is contributing to the instability.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Lateral ulnar collateral ligament (LUCL) reconstruction** — ligament repair for elbow stability
- **HemiCAP toe prosthesis** — off-label use of a small metal resurfacing implant for the capitellum
- **Box-loop ligament reconstruction** — for combined medial and lateral instability
