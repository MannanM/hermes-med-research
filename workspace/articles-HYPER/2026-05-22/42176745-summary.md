# Plain Language Summary: Return to Sport After Direct Anterior Approach Total Hip Arthroplasty

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42176745](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42176745/) | **Published:** 2026-05-22
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42176745.md) | [Full Text](./42176745-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This large survey study examined return-to-sport outcomes in 886 patients who underwent total hip replacement through the direct anterior approach (DAA), with minimum 5-year follow-up. The study assessed pre- and postoperative sport participation, time to return, and reasons for not returning.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** 74% of patients returned fully to prior activities, and 16% returned with modifications — 90% achieved this within one year.
- **Finding 2:** High-impact sport participation decreased from 24% preoperatively to 10% postoperatively; running decreased from 11% to 5%.
- **Finding 3:** Common reasons for not returning to running included surgeon advice (34%), fear of damaging the joint (21%), and pain (20%), though actual revision rates were similar between runners and non-runners (4.5% vs 3.2%).

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
Hip replacement is not uncommon in younger hypermobility patients who develop early joint degeneration. This study provides useful data on what to expect: most patients return to low-impact activities (walking, hiking, cycling) within a year, though high-impact activities decline. The finding that surgeon advice and fear — not actual implant failure — drive activity restriction is important for shared decision-making.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Direct anterior approach total hip arthroplasty (DAA THA)** — hip replacement through anterior approach
