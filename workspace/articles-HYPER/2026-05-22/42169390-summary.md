# Plain Language Summary: Delayed Diagnosis of Bilateral Posterior Shoulder Fracture-Dislocation Following a Seizure

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42169390](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42169390/) | **Published:** 2026-05-22
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42169390.md) | [Full Text](./42169390-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This case report describes a 27-year-old man whose bilateral posterior shoulder fracture-dislocations were missed for nearly a month after he had a seizure. Posterior shoulder dislocations are rare (2–5% of all shoulder dislocations) and are frequently misdiagnosed — 50–80% are missed at initial evaluation. The patient ultimately underwent successful open reduction and reconstruction surgery on both shoulders, achieving excellent functional recovery.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Bilateral posterior shoulder dislocation is extremely rare (<1% of posterior dislocations) and is strongly associated with seizure activity.
- **Finding 2:** Even with a delayed diagnosis (3+ weeks), anatomical surgical reconstruction combined with structured rehabilitation can achieve excellent outcomes if the rotator cuff is intact.
- **Finding 3:** The patient's Constant score improved from 4 (preoperative) to 95 (postoperative), indicating near-complete functional recovery.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
While this case is about seizure-related trauma, hypermobility patients are at increased risk for shoulder dislocations (often anterior rather than posterior). The key lesson: shoulder dislocations in hypermobility patients can also be subtle and easily missed. Proper imaging (axillary or scapular-Y views) is essential for diagnosis, and timely treatment is important to prevent chronic instability.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Open reduction** — surgical repositioning of the humeral head
- **Biceps tenodesis** — tendon repair/repositioning
- **Greater tuberosity fixation** — screw fixation of fractured bone
- **Subscapularis anchor repair** — soft tissue repair
- **Structured physiotherapy** — postoperative rehabilitation
