# Plain Language Summary: Open Versus Arthroscopic Triangular Fibrocartilage Repairs

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42186665](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42186665/) | **Published:** 2026-05-22
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42186665.md) | [Full Text](./42186665-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This narrative review compared open versus arthroscopic (keyhole) surgical repair of the triangular fibrocartilage complex (TFCC) — a structure in the wrist that stabilizes the forearm bones and transmits load across the wrist joint. Both surgical approaches were evaluated for indications, techniques, complications, and outcomes.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Both open and arthroscopic repairs yield similarly good mid-term patient-reported outcomes — neither approach is clearly superior.
- **Finding 2:** Arthroscopic repair may allow earlier recovery and carries a slightly lower soft-tissue complication risk.
- **Finding 3:** Open repair offers better visualization and is advantageous for complex or foveal (deep) TFCC tears.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
Wrist pain and instability are common in hypermobility/Ehlers-Danlos patients, and TFCC injuries may occur from minor trauma due to ligamentous laxity. This review suggests that for hypermobility patients with TFCC tears, either surgical approach can work — the choice should be individualized based on the specific tear pattern and surgeon expertise. Arthroscopic repair may be preferable for faster recovery in this often younger patient population.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Arthroscopic TFCC repair** — minimally invasive keyhole surgery
- **Open TFCC repair** — traditional open surgical approach
