# Plain Language Summary: Bilateral Traumatic Calcaneocuboid Joint Instability Treated with Modified Tunnel Technique

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42172357](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42172357/) | **Published:** 2026-05-22
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42172357.md) | [Full Text](./42172357-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This case report describes a 39-year-old woman who sustained simultaneous bilateral injuries to the calcaneocuboid joint (a joint in the midfoot connecting the heel bone to the cuboid bone). After conservative treatment failed, she underwent a surgical stabilization procedure on both feet using a synthetic fiber-tape construct to reconstruct the damaged ligaments.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** The patient's pain scores improved dramatically (VAS 8 to 2), and functional scores improved from 22.8% to 72.8% at 2-year follow-up.
- **Finding 2:** The modified tunnel technique using fiber-tape provided a simple, effective method for stabilizing the calcaneocuboid joint with minimal surgical morbidity.
- **Finding 3:** Simultaneous bilateral surgery was feasible and safe in this patient.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
Midfoot instability (including the calcaneocuboid joint) is a common but often overlooked problem in hypermobility patients who frequently have flat feet and generalized ligamentous laxity. This case demonstrates a surgical technique for addressing such instability when conservative management fails. The use of fiber-tape (synthetic ligament augmentation) may be particularly relevant for hypermobility patients whose native ligaments are inherently weak.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Calcaneocuboid joint reconstruction** — using fiber-tape with modified tunnel technique
- **Conservative management** — rest and non-surgical treatment (failed in this case)
