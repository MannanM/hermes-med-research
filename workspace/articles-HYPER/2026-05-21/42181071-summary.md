# Plain Language Summary: Social Determinants of Health Disparities and Outcomes After Bankart Repair

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42181071](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42181071/) | **Published:** 2026-05-21
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42181071.md) | [Full Text](./42181071-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This large database study examined how social determinants of health disparities (SDHD) — factors like income, education, neighborhood, and access to care — affect outcomes after Bankart repair surgery for recurrent shoulder dislocation. Using a database of over 142,000 patients, the researchers compared those with and without documented social/economic disadvantages.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Patients with SDHD had similar rates of medical complications after surgery but used more healthcare resources — they had higher rates of physical therapy use (51.6% vs 46.7%), more emergency department visits, and higher overall healthcare costs.
- **Finding 2:** SDHD patients were 49% more likely to develop shoulder stiffness within 2 years after surgery.
- **Finding 3:** SDHD patients took slightly longer to start physical therapy after surgery, which may contribute to worse long-term outcomes.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
Shoulder instability is one of the most common problems in hypermobility/Ehlers-Danlos patients, and Bankart repair is a surgical option when conservative management fails. This study highlights that social and economic barriers can significantly impact surgical outcomes — a consideration for hypermobility patients who may already face challenges accessing appropriate care. Ensuring timely access to physical therapy is critical for optimal recovery after shoulder stabilization surgery.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Bankart repair (open or arthroscopic)** — surgical repair for anterior shoulder instability
- **Physical therapy** — post-operative rehabilitation
