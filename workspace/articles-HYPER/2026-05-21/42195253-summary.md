# Plain Language Summary: Prevalence of Homologous Recombination Deficiency in Advanced Ovarian Cancer — Bulgaria

> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42195253.md) | [Full Text](./42195253-full.txt)

## Overview
This observational study examined the prevalence of homologous recombination deficiency (HRD) — a type of DNA repair defect — in 100 Bulgarian women with newly diagnosed advanced ovarian cancer. Researchers also described treatment patterns and genetic testing practices in this population.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** 58% of patients had HRD-positive tumors, with 34.5% of those carrying BRCA1/2 mutations.
- **Finding 2:** Most patients (98%) had good functional status at diagnosis, and standard platinum-based chemotherapy was the frontline treatment.
- **Finding 3:** HRD testing in Bulgaria is centralized and industry-supported, which may limit access.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
**Not applicable.** This article covers ovarian cancer genetics and treatment in Bulgaria, with no relevance to hypermobility disorders or Ehlers-Danlos syndrome.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **PARP inhibitors** — targeted therapy for HRD-positive ovarian cancer
- **Platinum-based chemotherapy** — standard frontline treatment
