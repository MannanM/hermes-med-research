# Plain Language Summary: Kinesiophobia, Global Health, Pain, and Bone Mineral Density in Women with Hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42168630](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42168630/) | **Published:** 2026-05-21
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42168630.md) | [Full Text](./42168630-full.txt)

## Overview
This study assessed the availability and quality of clinical practice guidelines (CPGs) for Ehlers-Danlos syndromes (EDS). Conducted by the European Reference Network for rare connective tissue diseases (ReCONNET), the authors performed a systematic search of medical literature databases to find any existing high-quality guidelines for diagnosing, monitoring, and treating EDS. They then evaluated what gaps and unmet needs remain for patients and clinicians.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Out of 193 initial search results, only 24 articles were identified as potentially containing guidelines — and none of them met the rigorous definition of a good-quality clinical practice guideline. There are currently NO evidence-based CPGs for EDS.
- **Finding 2:** Major unmet needs identified include: lack of prevalence data, no consensus on medical or surgical management, no clinically reliable biomarkers for diagnosis, limited provider education about EDS, and insufficient access to multidisciplinary care.
- **Finding 3:** The review confirmed that EDS remains "treatable but not curable," requiring multidisciplinary approaches (pain medication, physiotherapy, occupational therapy, orthotics, psychological support). Medical marijuana was noted as a promising alternative to opioids for pain, though not widely available.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
This is a sobering finding: despite the 2017 reclassification of EDS into 13+ subtypes, there are still no standardized, evidence-based guidelines to help doctors diagnose and treat these conditions. Patients often face long diagnostic delays, are dismissed by providers, and lack access to coordinated care. The article underscores the critical need for more research funding and structured care pathways for EDS.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Pain management:** Multidisciplinary care including pain medication, physiotherapy, occupational therapy, psychology
- **Orthotics and mobility aids:** To stabilize hypermobile joints and improve daily function
- **Medical marijuana:** Noted as promising alternative to opioids in clinical experience
- **Conservative treatment preferred** over surgery due to tissue fragility
- **Holistic/alternative approaches** (e.g., osteopathy) used as complementary therapy
