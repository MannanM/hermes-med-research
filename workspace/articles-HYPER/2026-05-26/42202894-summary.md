# Plain Language Summary: Static and Dynamic Internal Ulnar Rotational Stability in Baseball Pitchers

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42202894](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42202894/) | **Published:** 2026-05-26
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42202894.md) | [Full Text](./42202894-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This study examined the role of the posterior oblique ligament (POL) in stabilizing the elbow against internal rotational stress in 53 collegiate baseball pitchers. The researchers used ultrasound to measure changes in the elbow joint gap in pitchers with and without POL abnormalities, at rest and during muscle contraction.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** POL morphological abnormalities were present in 34% of asymptomatic pitchers.
- **Finding 2:** Pitchers with abnormal POLs had significantly larger elbow joint gaps under valgus stress (+0.63 mm more gapping).
- **Finding 3:** Activation of the elbow flexor muscles reduced joint gapping, suggesting that dynamic muscular stabilization can compensate for ligamentous laxity.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
This study directly relates to ligamentous laxity and dynamic stability — core concepts in hypermobility disorders. The finding that muscle activation can compensate for ligament insufficiency supports the importance of strengthening exercises in hypermobility patients. The concept that abnormal ligaments can be present without symptoms in athletes is also consistent with the hypermobility spectrum, where ligamentous laxity does not always cause symptoms.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
None — this is an observational biomechanical study.
