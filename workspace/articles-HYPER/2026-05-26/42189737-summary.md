# Plain Language Summary: Open Bankart Repair with Inferior Capsular Shift in Collision Athletes

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42189737](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42189737/) | **Published:** 2026-05-26
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42189737.md) | [Full Text](./42189737-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This editorial commentary discusses the ongoing clinical importance of open Bankart repair (with inferior capsular shift) for treating recurrent anterior shoulder instability in collision and contact athletes. While arthroscopic Bankart repair is more common, the authors argue that open repair retains an important role — particularly in athletes with subcritical bone loss and on-track Hill-Sachs lesions.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Open Bankart repair with inferior capsular shift produces excellent outcomes comparable to arthroscopic repair combined with remplissage in collision athletes.
- **Finding 2:** Postoperative subscapularis integrity and function are preserved with open repair, addressing a historical concern about the approach.
- **Finding 3:** The choice between open and arthroscopic techniques should be individualized based on the specific pattern of bone and soft tissue injury.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
Recurrent shoulder instability is extremely common in hypermobility/Ehlers-Danlos patients. This commentary is relevant because hypermobility patients often have multifactorial instability (ligamentous laxity plus potential bone defects) that may benefit from open surgical approaches with capsular shift, which address both the labral tear and the overall capsular laxity.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Open Bankart repair with inferior capsular shift** — surgical shoulder stabilization
- **Arthroscopic Bankart repair with remplissage** — minimally invasive alternative
