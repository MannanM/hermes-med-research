# Plain Language Summary: Internal Joint Stabilizer for Terrible Triad Elbow Injuries

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42202893](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42202893/) | **Published:** 2026-05-26
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42202893.md) | [Full Text](./42202893-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This study evaluated whether a device called the Internal Joint Stabilizer (IJS) — a temporary internal splint for the elbow — reduced the need for a second (medial) surgical incision when treating "terrible triad" elbow injuries (posterior dislocation plus fractures of the coronoid and radial head). The study compared 51 patients treated with the IJS to historical controls from the literature.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Only 3.9% of patients treated with the IJS required a supplemental medial approach, compared to a historical rate of 18–53% (average 38.3%).
- **Finding 2:** The risk of needing a medial approach was reduced by 90% with IJS use.
- **Finding 3:** The IJS provides enough stability from the lateral approach alone to avoid the additional morbidity of a medial incision.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
Elbow injuries (including dislocations) are relevant to hypermobility patients who have inherently lax elbow ligaments. The IJS is a temporary stabilization device that could be particularly useful in hypermobility patients with complex elbow injuries, where ligament healing may be slower and instability risk higher.

## Investigated Treatments / Interventions
- **Internal Joint Stabilizer (IJS)** — temporary internal elbow splint
- **Lateral surgical approach** — single-incision technique
- **Supplemental medial approach** — second incision (largely avoided with IJS)
