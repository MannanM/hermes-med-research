# Plain Language Summary: MET Exon 14 Skipping Mutations in Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42174600](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42174600/) | **Published:** 2026-05-23
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42174600.md) | [Full Text](./42174600-full.txt)

---

## Overview
This genomic study characterized MET exon 14 skipping mutations (METΔex14) — a driver mutation in non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). Analyzing 585 patients, researchers examined the genetic landscape, co-occurring mutations, and mechanisms of drug resistance to targeted therapies like crizotinib and savolitinib.

## Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** METΔex14 occurred in 1.02% of the NSCLC population screened, with higher prevalence in sarcomatoid histology.
- **Finding 2:** Common concurrent alterations included TP53 (40.8%), CDK4 (16%), and EGFR (12.4%).
- **Finding 3:** Resistance to MET inhibitors arose through on-target MET D1228X/Y1230X mutations or off-target alterations in RTK/RAS/MAPK and PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathways.

## What This Means for Hypermobility Patients
**Not applicable.** This article covers lung cancer genomics and targeted therapy resistance, with no relevance to hypermobility disorders or Ehlers-Danlos syndrome.
