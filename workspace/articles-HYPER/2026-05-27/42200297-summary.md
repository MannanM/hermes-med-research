# Plain Language Summary: ZFP998 Coordinates Naïve Pluripotency and Genomic Stability in Stem Cells

> **Original Study:** [PMID 42200297](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42200297/) | **Published:** 2026-05-27
> **Local Resources:** [Metadata](./42200297.md) | [Full Text](./42200297-full.txt)

## Overview
This basic science study identified a new regulator — ZFP998 — that simultaneously maintains naïve pluripotency and genomic stability in mouse embryonic stem cells. The researchers found that ZFP998 controls the expression of both pluripotency genes and DNA damage repair genes. When expressed in human stem cells, it enhanced both pluripotency and genomic stability.
