## Overview

Patients with hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (hEDS) and hypermobility spectrum disorders (HSD) frequently experience dysautonomia (autonomic nervous system dysfunction), affecting up to 70% of patients. Hypertension in this population presents unique diagnostic and management challenges because standard antihypertensives can paradoxically worsen orthostatic intolerance. This profile tracks medications that target specific hypertensive endotypes common in hypermobility disorders.

## Scientific Findings

### Update — 2026-05-21
**Study:** *Too High and Too Loose: Dysautonomia and the Hypertensive Paradox in Hypermobility Disorders* (PMID: [42186391](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42186391/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-HYPER/2026-05-21/42186391-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- Four distinct hypertensive endotypes identified in hEDS/HSD: hyperadrenergic dysautonomia, paroxysmal hypertension, cervicogenic hypertension, and nocturnal/supine hypertension.
- Standard antihypertensives (beta-blockers, diuretics) may worsen orthostatic intolerance in this population.
- Alpha-blockers (e.g., doxazosin, prazosin) and central sympatholytics (e.g., clonidine) show promise for the hyperadrenergic phenotype.
- Phenotype-directed pharmacotherapy is recommended: treatment should be matched to the specific hypertensive subtype.
- Avoidance of volume-depleting agents (diuretics) is advised.
