## Overview

Ehlers-Danlos syndromes (EDS) and hypermobility spectrum disorders (HSD) are multisystemic conditions requiring coordinated care across multiple medical specialties. Because EDS affects connective tissue throughout the body, optimal management involves rheumatology, cardiology, gastroenterology, pain management, physiotherapy, occupational therapy, psychology, and other specialties working together.

## Scientific Findings

### Update — 2026-05-21
**Study:** *Kinesiophobia, Global Health, Pain, and Bone Mineral Density in Women with Hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome* (PMID: [42168630](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42168630/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-HYPER/2026-05-21/42168630-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- The ERN ReCONNET systematic review confirmed that absolutely no high-quality clinical practice guidelines exist for EDS diagnosis, monitoring, or treatment.
- Major unmet needs include: coordinated multidisciplinary care, rehabilitation in expert centres, and access to knowledgeable providers.
- Pain management requires multidisciplinary input including pain medication, intensive physiotherapy, podiatry, psychology, occupational therapy, and adequate bracing.
- Conservative treatment is preferred over surgery due to tissue fragility.
- In many European countries and beyond, there are no diagnostic centres or experts available to patients.

### Update — 2026-05-21
**Study:** *Insights in Altered Eating Behaviors in Women with Ehlers-Danlos Syndromes* (PMID: [42165917](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42165917/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-HYPER/2026-05-21/42165917-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- Disordered eating in EDS is often secondary to GI problems, food allergies, and oral pain — not primarily psychiatric.
- Supports the need for integrated care addressing GI symptoms, pain management, and psychological support simultaneously.
