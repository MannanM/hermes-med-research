## Overview

Patellar instability (kneecap subluxation or dislocation) is one of the most common orthopedic problems in hypermobility/Ehlers-Danlos patients. Surgical management typically involves medial patellofemoral ligament (MPFL) reconstruction, but hypermobility patients often require additional bony procedures to address underlying malalignment, trochlear dysplasia, or abnormal patellar height.

## Scientific Findings

### Update — 2026-05-27
**Study:** *Beyond the Medial Patellofemoral Ligament: The Role of Concomitant Bony Procedures in Recurrent Patellar Instability* (PMID: [42202305](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42202305/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-HYPER/2026-05-27/42202305-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- MPFL reconstruction alone may not address all contributing factors — especially in patients with bony abnormalities.
- Adjunctive bony procedures include: tibial tubercle osteotomy (realignment), trochleoplasty (reshaping the femoral groove), and derotational distal femoral osteotomy (correcting rotational malalignment).
- A patient-specific approach integrating soft tissue and bony procedures is essential to reduce recurrence.
- Comprehensive preoperative evaluation of alignment, patellar height, and trochlear anatomy is recommended.
