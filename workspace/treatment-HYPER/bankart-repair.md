# Bankart Repair for HYPER

> Compiled: 2026-05-28
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Bankart repair is a surgical procedure to repair the torn labrum (the ring of cartilage around the shoulder socket) in patients with recurrent anterior shoulder dislocation. This is one of the most common orthopedic procedures relevant to hypermobility/Ehlers-Danlos patients, who are at significantly increased risk for shoulder instability due to generalized ligamentous laxity. Both open and arthroscopic techniques are used.

## Scientific Findings

### Update — 2026-05-21
**Study:** *Correlation of Social Determinants of Health Disparities With Outcomes After Bankart Repair* (PMID: [42181071](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42181071/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-HYPER/2026-05-21/42181071-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- In a study of 142,594 Bankart repairs, patients with social/economic disparities had similar medical complication rates but higher healthcare utilization.
- SDHD patients had a 49% higher risk of shoulder stiffness at 2 years post-surgery.
- Patients with social/economic barriers took longer to start PT and had more ED visits.
- Ensuring timely access to physical therapy is critical for optimal recovery.

### Update — 2026-05-26
**Study:** *Open Bankart Repair With Inferior Capsular Shift in Collision and Contact Athletes* (PMID: [42189737](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42189737/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-HYPER/2026-05-26/42189737-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- Open Bankart repair with inferior capsular shift retains clinical importance and produces outcomes comparable to arthroscopic repair with remplissage.
- Postoperative subscapularis integrity and function are preserved with open repair.
- For hypermobility patients, open repair with capsular shift may better address generalized ligamentous laxity than arthroscopic repair alone.
