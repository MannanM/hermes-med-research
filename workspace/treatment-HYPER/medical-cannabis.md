## Overview

Medical cannabis (medical marijuana) has been noted as a potentially valuable treatment option for chronic pain in Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (EDS). Pain in EDS is often severe, multifactorial, and poorly responsive to conventional treatments. Clinical experience reported in the EDS literature suggests cannabis may be a successful alternative to opioids, though availability varies by country.

## Scientific Findings

### Update — 2026-05-21
**Study:** *Kinesiophobia, Global Health, Pain, and Bone Mineral Density in Women with Hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome* (PMID: [42168630](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42168630/))
**Local Summary:** [Layperson Summary](../articles-HYPER/2026-05-21/42168630-summary.md)

**Findings:**
- Clinical practice guidelines for EDS are lacking; pain management remains driven by clinical experience.
- Medical cannabis was specifically noted as a promising alternative to opioids for EDS-related chronic pain.
- However, the review found no high-quality evidence-based guidelines for EDS treatment of any kind.
- Availability of medical cannabis varies: in many EU countries this treatment is not available.
