## Overview

⚠️ **Note: This article is NOT about MCAS.** It is a materials science paper about improving the efficiency and stability of perovskite solar cells. It was retrieved due to an unrelated PubMed match.

This study describes a new approach to making better solar cells by using two chemical additives that work together to improve the contacts between layers in the device. The method boosted energy conversion efficiency to over 25% and the cells retained 99% of their initial performance after 2,448 hours of dark storage.

## Key Findings

- A dual-additive strategy improved both crystal growth and defect repair in solar cell materials.
- Power conversion efficiencies reached 25.41% (small cells) and 22.18% (larger modules).
- Unencapsulated devices showed exceptional stability over extended storage.
