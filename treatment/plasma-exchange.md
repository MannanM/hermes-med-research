# Therapeutic Plasma Exchange (PEX) for ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-24 | Source: PubMed peer-reviewed case report  
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Therapeutic plasma exchange (PEX/plasmapheresis) is a procedure in which a patient's blood plasma is removed and replaced with donor plasma or a plasma substitute. It's used primarily in autoimmune and hematological disorders to remove pathogenic antibodies, cytokines, and other inflammatory mediators.

**Key findings:**

- Reports a case where ME/CFS developed **following SARS-CoV-2 infection**
- The patient was treated with **therapeutic plasma exchange (PEX)**
- The treatment appeared to be **successful** — suggesting this approach warrants further investigation
- The case also mentions **raltegravir** (an HIV integrase inhibitor) as part of the treatment regimen

**The authors state:**
> "Our successful case highlights the need to investigate novel therapeutic approaches, including plasma exchange."

## Mechanisms

Plasma exchange for ME/CFS is hypothesized to work through:

1. **Removal of autoantibodies** — Many ME/CFS patients have evidence of autoantibodies, particularly against β2-adrenergic and M3-muscarinic receptors
2. **Cytokine clearance** — Reduces the inflammatory cytokine burden
3. **Microclot removal** — Recent research has identified microclotting abnormalities in ME/CFS, particularly post-COVID
4. **Immune reset** — Removal of pathological immune complexes and restoration of normal immune function

## Clinical Context

Plasma exchange is:
- **Not currently a standard treatment** for ME/CFS
- A **significant medical procedure** requiring hospitalization and central venous access
- Associated with risks including infection, allergic reactions, hypocalcemia, and coagulopathy
- Typically reserved for severe, refractory cases in research settings
