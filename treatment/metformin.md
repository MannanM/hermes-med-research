# Metformin for ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-24 | Source: r/cfs community discussion  
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Metformin is a biguanide medication traditionally used as a first-line treatment for type 2 diabetes. It works primarily by reducing hepatic glucose production and improving insulin sensitivity. Interest in metformin for ME/CFS stems from its anti-inflammatory effects and potential impact on mitochondrial function and cellular energy metabolism.

## Community Discussion

### Metformin reducing PEM symptoms??

**Posted:** 2026-05-20 by u/Leilani__kai  
**Score:** 29 ↑ | **Comments:** 15  
**Source:** https://www.reddit.com/r/cfs/comments/1tijrph/metformin_reducing_pem_symptoms/  
**Flair:** Treatments

The user reported an unexpected finding: they were prescribed metformin **off-label** for weight management (not eligible for GLP-1 agonists like Zepbound/Wegovy). After about a week on metformin, they noticed a **significant reduction in PEM symptoms** — post-exertional malaise that would normally put them in bed for days was substantially milder.

**Key points from the discussion:**

- The user was **not pre-diabetic or diabetic** — metformin was prescribed purely off-label
- The improvement was **unexpected and not the intended effect**
- Community members discussed metformin's possible mechanisms:
  - **Anti-inflammatory effects** — metformin reduces TNF-α, IL-6, and other inflammatory cytokines
  - **Mitochondrial effects** — metformin activates AMPK, which regulates cellular energy homeostasis
  - **mTOR inhibition** — shares this mechanism with rapamycin, another drug being investigated for ME/CFS
- Some commenters shared similar positive experiences, others reported no effect
- Side effects mentioned: GI distress (common), caution for those with kidney issues

**Mechanism speculation from the thread:**
> Metformin is known to have anti-inflammatory properties beyond its glucose-lowering effects. It activates AMPK, which plays a central role in mitochondrial biogenesis and energy sensing. In ME/CFS, where mitochondrial dysfunction is well-documented, this could theoretically help.

## Known Research Context (Earlier Studies)

While not published this week, relevant prior work includes:

- **Metformin for Long COVID:** Several trials are ongoing (e.g., NCT04510194) examining metformin's role in preventing and treating post-COVID syndromes
- **AMPK activation in ME/CFS:** Metformin is one of several AMPK activators being explored for mitochondrial dysfunction in ME/CFS
- **mTOR pathway:** Metformin inhibits mTOR via AMPK activation, a pathway increasingly implicated in ME/CFS pathology
