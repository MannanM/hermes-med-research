## Overview

Low Dose Abilify (LDA) refers to the off-label use of aripiprazole — an atypical antipsychotic — at doses typically between 0.25–2mg (versus 10–30mg for psychiatric indications). At these low doses, aripiprazole is thought to act differently than at standard doses, potentially through dopaminergic modulation, anti-inflammatory effects, and mitochondrial impacts.

## LDA in ME/CFS Context

### Why Low Dose vs Standard Dose

At psychiatric doses (10–30mg), aripiprazole is a partial dopamine D2 agonist that stabilizes dopamine signaling. At low doses (0.25–2mg), the pharmacology is less clear but may involve:

1. **Dopamine D2 receptor partial agonism** — subtle modulation of dopamine pathways relevant to motivation and fatigue
2. **Anti-inflammatory effects** — reduces microglial activation and neuroinflammation
3. **5-HT2A receptor antagonism** — may improve sleep architecture
4. **BDNF upregulation** — brain-derived neurotrophic factor, potentially supporting neuronal health

### Evidence Base

- **Dr. Joseph "Jeff" Davis** (Stanford) and **Dr. Derek Enlander** (NY) pioneered LDA for ME/CFS
- No large RCTs exist — evidence is from case series and clinical practice
- **Reported response rate:** ~50-60% in some practitioner's clinical experience
- Benefits reported include: improved energy, reduced brain fog, better orthostatic tolerance
- **Effect onset:** Can be rapid (days) or gradual (weeks)

### Comparison: LDA vs LDN

| Aspect | LDA (Aripiprazole) | LDN (Naltrexone) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Typical dose | 0.25–2mg | 1–4.5mg |
| Cost | Generic, low cost | Compounding required, low cost |
| Onset | Days to weeks | Weeks |
| Common side effects | Akathisia (restlessness), insomnia | Vivid dreams, headache |
| Appetite effect | May increase cravings | Usually none |
| Weight changes | Possible weight gain | None |
| Access | Requires compounding or splitting tablets | Requires compounding |

### Side Effects to Watch

- **Akathisia** (feeling of inner restlessness/need to move) — most common reason for discontinuation
- **Insomnia**
- **Increased appetite/cravings**
- **Nausea**
- Less common: tardive dyskinesia (with prolonged use; risk lower at low doses)
