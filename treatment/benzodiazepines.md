# Benzodiazepines in ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-24 | Source: r/cfs community discussion  
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Benzodiazepines are GABA-A receptor positive allosteric modulators used for anxiety, insomnia, muscle spasms, and seizures. In the ME/CFS community, they present a significant dilemma: they can provide short-term relief for sleep, muscle tension, and anxiety, but carry serious risks of tolerance, dependence, and withdrawal — which can dramatically worsen ME/CFS symptoms.

## About Cinolazepam (Gerodorm)

- A long-acting benzodiazepine used as a hypnotic (sleep aid)
- Available primarily in Europe/Central Europe
- 40mg is a **high dose** (typical range: 15-40mg)
- Long half-life (~4-6 hours active, longer metabolites) — may accumulate

## The Benzodiazepine Paradox in ME/CFS

### Why They Can Seem Helpful Initially

1. **Muscle relaxation** — reduces the muscle tension and spasms common in ME/CFS
2. **Sleep promotion** — can induce sleep quickly
3. **Anxiety relief** — reduces the emotional distress of living with severe illness
4. **Seizure threshold** — some ME/CFS patients have comorbid seizure disorders

### Why They Can Be Dangerous

1. **Tolerance develops rapidly** — higher doses needed to maintain effect
2. **Withdrawal can mimic/permanently worsen ME/CFS** — fatigue, muscle pain, cognitive dysfunction, sleep disturbance
3. **Kindling** — repeated withdrawal episodes can sensitize the brain
4. **Respiratory depression risk** — especially combined with other sedatives
5. **Paradoxical reactions** — some ME/CFS patients report worsening fatigue or agitation
6. **Long-term use associated with** — increased disability, cognitive decline, falls, dementia risk in older adults

### The Tapering Challenge

Withdrawal from benzodiazepines can produce symptoms that are **nearly indistinguishable from ME/CFS** — profound fatigue, muscle pain, cognitive dysfunction, sleep disturbance, and PEM-like crashes. This makes it extremely difficult to distinguish between:
- Withdrawal symptoms
- The underlying ME/CFS
- A PEM crash triggered by overexertion during withdrawal

## Safer Alternatives for Sleep/Anxiety in ME/CFS

| Alternative | Notes |
|-------------|-------|
| **Gabapentin / Pregabalin** | May help sleep, pain, anxiety with less dependence risk |
| **Trazodone** | Non-addictive sleep aid; can help with mood |
| **Hydroxyzine** | Antihistamine; also helps MCAS; mild anxiolytic |
| **Magnesium glycinate** | Muscle relaxation; safe; non-addictive |
| **Low dose mirtazapine** | Improves sleep and appetite at low doses |
| **DORAs** (Quviviq, Dayvigo) | Sleep without GABA involvement; expensive |
| **L-theanine** | Calming amino acid; safe, over-the-counter |
| **Ashwagandha** | Adaptogen; can help with stress response |

## Current Status

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Prescribing trend | Decreasing in ME/CFS as risks become better known |
| Short-term use | May be appropriate in controlled circumstances (e.g., diagnostic procedures) |
| Long-term use | **Strongly discouraged** by most ME/CFS specialists |
| Tapering | Must be very slow (months to years); rushed tapering can cause severe harm |
| Community advice | "If you're on them, don't stop suddenly — but do have a plan to slowly wean off with medical supervision" |
