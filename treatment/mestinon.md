# Mestinon (Pyridostigmine) for POTS and ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-24 | Source: r/cfs community discussion  
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Mestinon (pyridostigmine bromide) is an **acetylcholinesterase inhibitor** that increases the availability of acetylcholine at the neuromuscular junction and throughout the autonomic nervous system. It's FDA-approved for myasthenia gravis but increasingly used off-label for POTS (postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome) and related autonomic dysfunction in ME/CFS.

## Why Mestinon May Help in ME/CFS

### Mechanism

1. **Acetylcholinesterase inhibition** — prevents breakdown of acetylcholine at synapses
2. **Enhanced vagal tone** — improves parasympathetic nervous system function (often impaired in ME/CFS)
3. **Improved orthostatic tolerance** — enhances baroreflex sensitivity and reduces tachycardia on standing
4. **Muscle function** — improved neuromuscular transmission, which may help with the muscle weakness component
5. **Cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway** — increased acetylcholine signaling reduces systemic inflammation

### Compared to Other POTS Medications

| Medication | Mechanism | User's Experience | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| **Mestinon** | Acetylcholinesterase inhibitor | "By far the most helpful" | Improves OI + muscle aching |
| **Propranolol** | Beta-blocker | Still taking | Reduces heart rate, may worsen fatigue |
| **Guanfacine** | Alpha-2 agonist | Still taking | Reduces sympathetic outflow |
| **Ivabradine** | If channel blocker | ❌ Didn't work | Heart rate-specific, no effect on blood pressure |
| **Florinef** | Mineralocorticoid (volume expansion) | ❌ Didn't work | Increases blood volume |
| **Midodrine** | Alpha-1 agonist (vasoconstrictor) | ❌ Didn't work | Increases blood pressure |

## Dosing & Practical Info

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Starting dose | 30-60mg, 2-3 times daily (immediate release) |
| Timing | Usually dosed every 4-6 hours |
| Onset | Within 30-60 minutes |
| Duration | 3-4 hours per dose |
| Extended release | Available (Mestinon Timespan) — 180mg once daily |

### Common Side Effects

- **Increased GI motility** — abdominal cramping, diarrhea (most common reason for discontinuation)
- **Excessive sweating**
- **Salivation / tearing**
- **Muscle fasciculations**
- **Bradycardia at high doses**

> Tip: Taking with food can reduce GI side effects. Some patients start with a low dose (30mg) and increase gradually.

## Evidence Context

- Several studies show pyridostigmine improves **heart rate and symptoms** in POTS (e.g., Raj et al., *Journal of Cardiovascular Pharmacology*)
- Mestinon is **not typically a first-line** POTS treatment (beta-blockers, ivabradine, fludrocortisone, midodrine come first)
- In ME/CFS specifically, the benefit is primarily through **orthostatic tolerance** rather than directly on fatigue/PEM
- The user's ranking of mestinon as "by far the most helpful" among the POTS meds is notable
