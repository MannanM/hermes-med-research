## Overview

Peptide therapy refers to the use of short chains of amino acids (peptides) to modulate specific biological pathways. A growing number of people with ME/CFS, Long COVID, and related conditions are exploring peptides, often self-administered, despite limited formal research for these conditions. Commonly discussed peptides include BPC-157, TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4), KPV, MOTS-c, and others.

## Peptides Commonly Discussed for ME/CFS

### BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157)
- **Claimed use:** Gut healing, tissue repair, systemic anti-inflammatory
- **Route:** Oral or injectable
- **ME/CFS relevance:** May help with leaky gut / gut permeability (common in ME/CFS); some report improved energy
- **Evidence:** Robust animal data for tissue healing; very limited human data for ME/CFS

### TB-500 / Thymosin Beta-4
- **Claimed use:** Tissue repair, anti-inflammatory, cardiac healing
- **Route:** Injectable
- **ME/CFS relevance:** Cell migration and repair signaling
- **Evidence:** Veterinary/athletic recovery use; no ME/CFS-specific studies

### KPV (Lysine-Proline-Valine)
- **Claimed use:** Anti-inflammatory, gut healing, MCAS stabilization
- **Route:** Oral or injectable
- **ME/CFS relevance:** Tripeptide derived from α-MSH; NF-κB inhibition

### MOTS-c (Mitochondrial Open Reading Frame of the 12S rRNA-c)
- **Claimed use:** Metabolic regulation, mitochondrial function
- **Route:** Injectable
- **ME/CFS relevance:** Directly targets mitochondrial function — highly relevant to ME/CFS pathophysiology
- **Evidence:** Early research in metabolic health; no ME/CFS trials

### Thymosin Alpha-1
- **Claimed use:** Immune modulation, infection resistance
- **Route:** Injectable
- **ME/CFS relevance:** May help with immune dysregulation in ME/CFS; used for chronic infections

## Key Issues in the Community

1. **Self-treatment is common** — Many users source peptides from online vendors and self-administer without medical supervision
2. **Grey market sourcing** — Most peptides are not FDA-approved; quality varies dramatically between suppliers
3. **No ME/CFS-specific studies** — Evidence is extrapolated from other conditions (wound healing, animal studies, anecdotal reports)
4. **Cost** — Can be $100-300+/month depending on peptide and dose
5. **Safety concerns** — Sterility, dosing errors, drug interactions, lack of purity testing
6. **MCAS caution** — Many people with ME/CFS have MCAS and may react to injected compounds
