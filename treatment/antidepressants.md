# Antidepressants (SSRIs/SNRIs) for ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-24 | Source: r/cfs community discussion  
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Antidepressants — particularly SSRIs (selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors) and SNRIs (serotonin-norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors) — are a deeply controversial topic in the ME/CFS community. While often prescribed for fatigue and pain, many patients report worsening of symptoms. The relationship between ME/CFS and medication for comorbid depression/anxiety is complex.

## Community Experiences (from 22 comments)

### Negative Experiences Reported
- Some users report SSRIs (particularly citalopram/escitalopram) made fatigue **significantly worse**
- **Abilify/aripiprazole** at low doses mentioned as a mood alternative
- **Wellbutrin/bupropion** (NDRI) often preferred over SSRIs because it's more activating (less sedating)
- Some users experienced **permanent worsening** — unable to return to pre-medication baseline

### Positive/Mixed Experiences Reported
- SNRIs (duloxetine/Cymbalta, venlafaxine/Effexor) helped some with pain and mood without worsening fatigue
- **Mirtazapine** (Remeron) helped some with sleep and appetite at low doses
- **Bupropion/Wellbutrin** was the most commonly cited "safe" option
- Several users emphasized: **start at the lowest possible dose** and titrate extremely slowly

## Relevant Research Context

### Study #8 from This Week's Roundup

**"Longitudinal association of mental-health problems with subsequent diagnosis of persistent somatic symptom disorders"**  
Kitselaar et al. (2026) — *General Hospital Psychiatry*  
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42167072/

Examined 11,409 patients and found associations between preceding mental health issues and subsequent IBS, CFS, and fibromyalgia diagnoses. Important context: this study looked at **diagnostic pathways** and classification, not treatment response.

## Key Takeaways for ME/CFS Patients Considering Antidepressants

| Consideration | Detail |
|---------------|--------|
| **SSRIs** | Mixed — many report worsened fatigue; some benefit for mood without energy cost |
| **SNRIs** (duloxetine, venlafaxine) | May help with pain + mood; less sedating than SSRIs |
| **Bupropion/Wellbutrin** (NDRI) | Most commonly reported as "safe" — activating, no serotonin involvement |
| **Mirtazapine** | Helpful for sleep/appetite at low doses; can cause weight gain |
| **General rule** | Start extremely low, titrate very slowly, monitor for PEM/baseline changes |
| **Psychiatrist selection** | Seek someone familiar with ME/CFS — standard approaches may be harmful |

> **A recurring theme in the thread:** "The depression from having ME/CFS is fundamentally different from primary depression, and treating it as if it's the same thing can backfire."

## Current Status

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Evidence level | **Poor** — no quality RCTs examining antidepressants in ME/CFS specifically |
| Guidelines | NICE guidelines (2021) advise caution with antidepressants, noting limited evidence |
| Community consensus | Very cautious approach; bupropion and low-dose SNRIs preferred over SSRIs |
| Risk | Some users report permanent worsening; mechanism unclear |
