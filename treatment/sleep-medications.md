# Sleep Medications for ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-24 | Source: r/cfs community discussion  
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Sleep disturbance is one of the most pervasive and debilitating symptoms of ME/CFS. Unlike simple insomnia, ME/CFS-related sleep problems often involve **unrefreshing sleep** — patients may sleep 8-10 hours but wake feeling completely unrestored. Many people with ME/CFS cycle through multiple sleep medications over the course of their illness.

This week's discussion focused on **Quviviq (daridorexant)** — a newer dual orexin receptor antagonist (DORA).

## About Quviviq (Daridorexant)

Quviviq is a **dual orexin receptor antagonist (DORA)** that blocks the action of orexin — a neuropeptide that promotes wakefulness. Unlike benzodiazepines or Z-drugs that act on GABA receptors, DORAs promote sleep through a different mechanism.

**Pros:**
- **Less dependency risk** than benzodiazepines/Z-drugs
- **No tolerance** in the traditional sense (not GABA-ergic)
- May produce more **physiological sleep architecture**
- Lower risk of falls / cognitive impairment than benzos

**Cons:**
- **Very expensive** (~$300-400+/month without insurance)
- Insurance may require prior authorization
- Some users report headaches, next-day sedation
- Can worsen depression in some patients
- May interact with CYP3A4 inhibitors

## Common Sleep Medications Used in ME/CFS

| Medication | Class | Pros | Cons |
|-----------|-------|------|------|
| **Trazodone** | SARI | Low dose effective; improves sleep architecture | Next-day sedation; anticholinergic at high doses |
| **Mirtazapine** | NaSSA | Helps sleep + appetite at low doses | Weight gain; morning grogginess |
| **Gabapentin** | Gabapentinoid | Also helps pain/RLS | Tolerance develops; withdrawal possible |
| **Pregabalin** | Gabapentinoid | Similar to gabapentin, stronger | Dependence; weight gain |
| **Cyclobenzaprine** | Muscle relaxant | Helps muscle tension + sleep | Anticholinergic; tolerance |
| **Low dose amitriptyline** | Tricyclic | Classic first-line; helps pain + sleep | Anticholinergic; morning grogginess |
| **Melatonin** | Hormone | Safe, over-the-counter | Works poorly for most ME/CFS patients |
| **Hydroxyzine** | Antihistamine | Also helps MCAS; safe | Next-day sedation; tolerance |
| **Benzodiazepines** | GABA-A agonist | Effective short-term | Tolerance, dependence, withdrawal worsening |
| **DORAs** (Quviviq, Dayvigo, Belsomra) | Orexin antagonist | Novel mechanism; less dependence | Very expensive; limited long-term data |

---

## Key Considerations for ME/CFS

- **Unrefreshing sleep is a diagnostic criterion** — no medication fully addresses this
- ME/CFS patients may be **more sensitive** to sedative medications (lower doses often work)
- **PEM recovery sleep** is different from regular sleep — may require different approaches during crashes
- Many patients find **combinations work** (e.g., melatonin + trazodone)
- **Circadian rhythm disruption** common — chronotherapy (timed light exposure, sleep scheduling) may help

---

## Current Status

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Best evidence | Trazodone and low-dose tricyclics (amitriptyline) have most evidence in ME/CFS |
| Quviviq | Newer option, less studied in ME/CFS specifically; patient experience mixed |
| Community consensus | No single medication works for most; highly individual |
| Cry for help common | "I just want to sleep" is a frequent theme — sleep deprivation compounds all ME/CFS symptoms |
