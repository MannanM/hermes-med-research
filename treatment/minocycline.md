# Minocycline for ME/CFS

> Compiled: 2026-05-24 | Source: r/cfs community discussion + linked research  
> **Disclaimer:** This is not medical advice. Consult a healthcare professional before trying any treatment.

---

## Overview

Minocycline is a second-generation tetracycline antibiotic with well-documented anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties independent of its antimicrobial effects. It crosses the blood-brain barrier readily and has been studied in various neurological and neuroinflammatory conditions.

## Research Context

### Japanese Pilot Study (2024)

**"Oral minocycline therapy as first-line treatment in patients with Myalgic encephalomyelitis and long COVID: A pilot study"**

- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39720104/
- A pilot study from Japan investigating minocycline as a **first-line treatment** for ME/CFS and Long COVID
- Built on an earlier 2021 study from the same group

### Earlier Study (2021)

- The 2021 study had a **~40% dropout rate**, suggesting tolerability issues
- Despite this, the results were promising enough to warrant further investigation

### Why Minocycline Might Work

1. **Anti-inflammatory** — Inhibits microglial activation and reduces pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6)
2. **Neuroprotective** — Reduces excitotoxicity and oxidative stress in the CNS
3. **Matrix metalloproteinase (MMP) inhibitor** — MMP dysregulation has been implicated in ME/CFS
4. **Mitochondrial effects** — Shown to stabilize mitochondrial function in some contexts

## Community Experiences

From the Reddit discussion:

- **Mixed experiences** — Some users reported improvement, others no change or negative effects
- **Side effects mentioned:**
  - Photosensitivity (common with tetracyclines)
  - GI upset
  - Dizziness/vertigo (minocycline-specific vestibular side effects)
  - Tooth discoloration with long-term use
- **Duration** — Some users reported needing several weeks before noticing any benefit
- **Dosing** — Typical doses discussed ranged from 100-200mg/day

**Notable comment themes:**
> "It helped my brain fog more than my physical fatigue."
> "I had to stop due to the dizziness — felt like I had motion sickness constantly."
> "Didn't work for me but I've seen others swear by it."
